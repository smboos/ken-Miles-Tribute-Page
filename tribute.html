Ken Miles

The Real Champion of 1966 Le Mans

Ken Miles was a British sports car racing engineer, driver and inductee to the Motorsports Hall of Fame of America.

Here are some fun facts about Mr. Miles

  • 1918 - Born in Sutton Coldfield, England.
  • 1942 - Promted to staff sergeant in the British Territorial Army.
  • 1944 - Stationed in a tank unit that took part in the Normandy landings.
  • 1952 - Moved to the US and settled in Los Angeles, California as a service manager for Gough Industries.
  • 1953 - won 14 straight victories in SCCA racing in an MG-based special of his own design and construction.
  • 1955 - Designed, constructed and campaigned a second special based on MG components that was known as the "Flying Shingle".
  • 1963 - Became the chief test driver of Shelby-American.
  • 1965 - Shared a Ford GT Mk.II with Bruce McLaren at the 24 Hours of Le Mans but retired with gearbox trouble.
  • 1966 - Was leading the 1966 24 Hours of Le Mans, but Ford executive Leo Beebe, desiring a publicity photo of three of their cars crossing the finish line together, instructed Carroll Shelby to order him to slow down.
  • 1966 - Died when the Ford J-car he was testing at Riverside International Raceway crashed and caught fire.
